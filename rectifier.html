Rectifier

Normally, homes receive Alternating Current (AC) from the switchboard, but commonly used devices like fridges, TVs, and mobile phone chargers work on Direct Current (DC). If these devices receive AC, they could get damaged. Hence, rectifiers are used to convert AC to DC in all such devices.

Convert AC --> DC


1. Half-Wave Rectifiers: Suitable for devices that don't require a steady DC output, such as some small household devices.
2. Full-Wave Rectifiers: Commonly used in DC power supplies for electronic devices because they provide a smooth and consistent power supply.
3. Bridge Rectifiers: Preferred in industrial power supplies due to their high efficiency and ability to handle high current loads.

Rectifier: A rectifier is an electronic device that converts alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC). This process is called rectification. Rectifiers are commonly used in power supplies for most electronic devices and appliances, as DC is the type of current needed for their proper operation.
