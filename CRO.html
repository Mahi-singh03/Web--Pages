CRO and CRT

{ CRO jo hai oh CRT to mil ke baneya hunda }

Definition of CRO:
A Cathode Ray Oscilloscope (CRO) is an electronic device used to display and analyze the shape of electrical signals. It helps in visualizing how voltage signals change over time.
Principle of CRO:
A CRO works by directing an electron beam across a screen using electric fields. The beam's path is altered by the voltage of the input signal. When the beam hits the screen, it lights up, displaying the signal as a visual waveform.

Block Diagram consist of Following parts:

  1. Input Signal: The electrical signal to be analyzed is fed into the CRO. Vertical Amplifier: This component amplifies the input signal, making it strong enough to be displayed on the screen.
  2. Delay Line: This delays the signal slightly to ensure that the horizontal sweep starts before the signal reaches the screen, allowing the beginning of the signal to be seen.
  3. Input Signal: The electrical signal to be analyzed is fed into the CRO. Vertical Amplifier: This component amplifies the input signal, making it strong enough to be displayed on the screen.
  4. Electron Gun: Generates a focused beam of electrons directed towards the screen.
  5. Vertical Deflection Plates: These plates control the vertical movement of the electron beam based on the amplified input signal, representing the signal's amplitude.
  6. Horizontal Deflection Plates: These plates control the horizontal movement of the electron beam, creating the time base for the signal.
  7. Cathode Ray Tube (CRT): The main display component where the electron beam creates a visible trace of the signal on a phosphorescent screen. Screen: Displays the waveform of the input signal.
  8. Screen: Displays the waveform of the input signal.
  9. Trigger Circuit: Synchronizes the time base generator with the input signal, ensuring a stable and clear waveform display.
  10. Time Base Generator: Generates a ramp voltage that controls the horizontal deflection plates, providing the time axis for the waveform display.
  11. Horizontal Amplifier: Amplifies the ramp signal from the time base generator to control the horizontal deflection of the electron beam.
  12. Provides the necessary voltages to all the components within the CRO

Working of CRO:
The CRO operates by first amplifying the input signal through the vertical amplifier and then passing it through a delay line to ensure the entire waveform is visible. The trigger circuit synchronizes the signal with the horizontal sweep generated by the time base generator, which creates a ramp voltage controlling the horizontal deflection plates. This ramp voltage is then amplified by the horizontal amplifier. Simultaneously, the vertical deflection plates adjust according to the input signal's amplitude. An electron gun emits a beam of electrons that is directed by these deflection plates to move across the phosphorescent screen of the Cathode Ray Tube (CRT). The screen lights up where the electron beam strikes, displaying the waveform of the input signal. The power supply provides necessary voltages to all components, ensuring smooth operation.

Definition of CRT:
A Cathode Ray Tube (CRT) is an electronic device that uses electron beams to create images on a phosphorescent screen.
Principle of CRT:
The principle of a CRT is based on the behavior of electrons. An electron gun emits a beam of electrons, which are then directed by electric or magnetic fields. These electrons move across a phosphorescent screen, causing it to emit light upon impact. The movement of the beam and its intensity are controlled to form images.
Cunstruction/Block-Diagram:
Block Diagram consist of Following parts:

  1. Electron Gun: It generates and accelerates a beam of electrons.
  2. Focusing and Accelerating Anodes: These elements focus and speed up the electrons towards the screen.
  3. Deflection Plates/Coils: These steer the electron beam horizontally and vertically.
  4. Phosphorescent Screen: This screen glows when struck by the electron beam, creating a visible image.
  5. Glass Enclosure: The entire setup is enclosed in a vacuum-sealed glass tube to prevent electron scattering.

Working of CRO:
In a CRT, the electron gun generates an electron beam that is focused and accelerated towards the screen. The deflection plates or coils control the direction of the electron beam. When the beam strikes the phosphorescent screen, the screen glows at the point of impact, creating a spot of light. By rapidly moving the beam across the screen in a controlled manner, an image is formed. The intensity of the electron beam can be varied to change the brightness of the spots on the screen, enabling the display of different shades and contrasts.
